How Contagion HMPV

Human metapneumovirus (HMPV) spreads through respiratory droplets from coughs, sneezes, or contact with contaminated surfaces. Highly contagious, it causes cold-like symptoms, pneumonia, and bronchitis, especially in young children and older adults. Prevention includes handwashing and avoiding close contact with infected individuals.

Prevention HMPV virus

Prevent HMPV by washing hands, avoiding close contact, disinfecting surfaces, covering coughs, wearing masks, maintaining hygiene, boosting immunity, staying hydrated, avoiding crowded places, and seeking medical advice if symptoms appear.

  • Wash Your Hands For 20sec

    Wash your hands with soap and water for 20 seconds to remove germs, prevent infections and protect yourself and others.

  • Cover Nose and Mouth When Sneezing

    Cover your nose and mouth with a tissue or elbow when sneezing to prevent the spread of germs, protect others, and maintain hygiene. Dispose of tissues properly.

  • Wear A Mask If Available

    Wear a mask to prevent infections, protect others, reduce germ spread, especially in crowds, and stay safe from viruses like HMPV and flu.
